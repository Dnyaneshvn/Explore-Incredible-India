In India, life is synonymous with festivals because there are more fiestas than one can count, and each of these mirrors our culture and traditions.India is a land of festivals, where people from different religions coexist harmoniously. The wide variety of festivals celebrated in India is a true manifestation of its rich culture and traditions.

Almost every big and small occasion in India calls for a celebration , and each festival in India has an essence and flavor of its own. Be it the arrival of spring, harvesting of crops or something else. So, every day is a new celebration in this country.

Famous Festivals of India





Diwali

Diwali is a festival of lights and one of the major festivals of India.People decorating their homes and workplaces with diyas and rangolis during this Festival.

Durga Puja

Durga Puja is a Hindu festival which reveres and pays homage to the Hindu goddess Durga and is also celebrated because of Durga's victory over Mahishasur.

Dussehra

Dussehra is a major Hindu festival celebrated at the end of Navaratri every year to remember god Rama's victory over Ravana.

Ganesh Chaturthi

Ganesh Chaturthi is a Hindu festival celebrating the arrival of Lord Ganesh to earth from Kailash Parvat with his mother Goddess Parvati.

Rakshabandhan

On this day, sisters tie a amulet, called the Rakhi, around the wrists of their brothers, symbolically protecting them.

Holi

Holi is a popular ancient Indian festival, also known as the "Festival of Colours"

Vaisakhi

Vaisakhi is a major Sikh festival marks the birth of the Khalsa order by Guru Gobind Singh, the tenth Guru of Sikhism, on Vaisakhi of 1699.

Lohri

Lohri is a popular winter folk festival celebrated primarily in the Indian Subcontinent.It is believed that the festival marks the passing of the winter solstice
