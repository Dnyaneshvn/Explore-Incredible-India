India's shorelines are endowed with a glorious beauty that is reflected in its undulating golden beaches that make for an ideal vantage point to watch the sun dipping into the sea. Beaches stroke both the eastern and western coasts of the Indian peninsula. They also act as sentinels of the rich cultural heritage of India and host many temples, memorials and monuments. Several beaches in the country are sites of adventure sports like skiing, scuba diving, parasailing and windsurfing. India's coastal line lures many migratory birds and aquatic life to come and nestle on the sun-kissed shores.

Popular Beaches in India

Kovalam Beach

Since as early as the 1930s, Kovalam Beach has been an internationally famous stopover for tourists. There are three crescent-shaped beaches with a rock wall that make Kovalam one of the best spots in the country for sea swimming. You can also go on a catamaran cruise or sunbathes.

Colva Beach

Colva Beach is one of the most popular beaches in the state and is located in South Goa.

Vagator Beach

Among Goa's most photographed beaches, Vagator Beach is situated on the Caisua Bay along River Chapora.

Anjuna Beach

Renowned for its serene vistas of swaying palms, golden sand and the unusual rock formations, Anjuna Beach is located about 18 km away from Panaji.

Calangute Beach

Fondly called the queen of beaches by locals, Calangute Beach is among the most popular beaches in Goa, attracting large crowds throughout the year.

Palolem Beach

Palolem is arguably Goa's most picturesque beach. It's a mile-long, semi-circle shaped beach enclosed by a thick forest of coconut palms.
