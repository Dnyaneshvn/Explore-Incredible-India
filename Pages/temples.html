India is known to be one of the oldest civilizations in the world and is known to be the origin of few of the most important religions of the world including Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism and Sikhism. With over thousands of rich heritage, culture and traditions, India has thousands of temples dedicated to hundreds of gods.

Some of the temples have been in existence for more than a 1,000 years and some temples have been built only in the last decade, yet their importance varies from place to place.Visiting any of these famous temples would take you to a journey of faith, spirituality and a lot of cultural heritage that you won't witness anywhere else.

Famous Temples in India





Ram Janma Bhumi

Ram Mandir is a Hindu temple that is being built in Ayodhya,the birthplace of Rama, an incarnation of god Vishnu.

Siddhivinayak Temple

Siddhivinayak is a Lord Ganesha temple, one of the most significant and frequented temples in Mumbai. It was built in the year 1801.

Dwarkadheesh Temple

Having a history of over 2500 years, this Chalukya style of architecture is a temple dedicated to Lord Krishna.

Jagannath Temple

This temple dating back to 12th century, enshrines Lord Jagannath, Devi Subhadra and Balabhadra and is among one of the most famous temples.

Kedarnath Temple

Kedarnath is the highest among the 12 Jyotirlingas. This temple, over a thousand years old, is built of enormous stone slabs.

Kashi Vishwanath

Located in the heart of Varanasi,Kashi Vishwanath Temple is one of the most popular Indian temples dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Somnath Temple

The Somnath Temple is first of the twelve Jyotirlinga shrines of Lord Shiva. It is located on the western coast of Gujarat.

Sun Temple

Dedicated to Lord Surya, this 13th century temple is an architectural marvel and the quintessential example of Oriyan form of architecture.

Mata Vaishno Devi Mandir

Located in Trikuta hills, 13 km from Katra is the holy cave temple of Mata Vaishnodevi, with spirituality lingering in the atmosphere.

Krishna Janma Bhumi

Shri Krishna Janmasthan Temple is of great significance for the Hindus as it is the birthplace of Lord Krishna.
