India is blessed with heritage riches and a regal lineage. Vestiges of Indian royalty can be felt in the various forts and palaces speckled across the country. From hill forts in Rajasthan to formidable fortifications in Gwalior, Golconda and Delhi, the history of India stands tall and proud, housed inside these marvellous structures. To experience how the royalty lived in the past, several of these palace-turned heritage hotels are a must-visit.

Visiting these forts and palaces is the best way to uncover a real treasure of Indian history. These serve as great reminders of the bygone eras, mystical kingdoms and their rulers.

Popular Forts And Palaces In India

Agra Fort

Built by Mughal emperor Akbar in 1565 AD, Agra Fort is a majestic sandstone built as an ode to the magnificence of the Mughal empire. Encompassing within its 2.5-km-long enclosure walls a stunning imperial city, the fortress is shaped like a crescent, its eastern wall flanked by River Yamuna.

Amber Fort

Just 11 km outside Jaipur, lies the magnificent Amber Fort (Amer Fort), sitting atop a hill, and rising like a sentinel guarding the city

City Palace

Located in the heart of old Jaipur, the City Palace is a spectacular example of the amalgamation of Rajput and Mughal architecture.

City Palace

Rising from the bank of Lake Pichola, the imposing City Palace is a grand icon of Udaipur's and Rajasthan's royal past.

Jaisalmer Fort

The piece de resistance of the lively Thar desert is a magnificent structure built from yellow sandstone, called the Jaisalmer Fort.
